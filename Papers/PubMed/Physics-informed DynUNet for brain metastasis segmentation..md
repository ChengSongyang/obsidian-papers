## 摘要
BACKGROUND: In neuro-oncology, detecting, segmenting, and delineating the boundaries of small-volume brain metastatic foci remains a significant challenge. The lack of explicit biological information on metastasis growth and spread in standard deep learning architectures further limits low-volume metastatic lesions. This study investigates whether integrating physics-informed (PI) tumor growth models into segmentation architectures can overcome these size-dependent limitations. METHODS: Using the BraTS-METS 2023 dataset, we integrated a physics-based growth model with DynUNet to construct PI-DynUNet and compared it with three U-Net variants under controlled conditions. All models were trained on the same data without data augmentation, using matched parameter counts, identical hyperparameters, and deterministic settings. We compared seven physics regularization weights (λ) with 5-fold cross-validation and evaluated performance in six lesion-size categories using Dice, IoU and HD95. To assess clinical context-specific performance, we calculated scenario-weighted Dice coefficients for RANO progression assessment, radiotherapy planning, and surgical decision-making. RESULTS: PI-DynUNet achieved effective metastasis segmentation across all BraTS regions. Relative to baseline DynUNet, it improved whole tumor (WT) Dice by 1.8 %, tumor core (TC) Dice by 2.5 %, and enhancing tumor (ET) Dice by 2.6 %. For the challenging non-enhancing tumor core (NETC), Dice increased by 5.3 %. Optimal regularization weights depended on tissue type and lesion size: λ = 1.0 favored extensive edema and whole-tumor regions, λ = 0.01 best served large contrast-enhancing tumors and necrotic cores. Scenario-weighted evaluation revealed context-dependent optimal models: PI-DynUNet (λ = 0.01) excelled in enhancing-weighted scenarios (RANO: +2.6 %; RT-GTV: +2.2 % vs. baseline), while λ = 1.0 demonstrated superior balanced accuracy (RT-CTV: +1.8 %; Surgical: +1.6 %). CONCLUSIONS: Physics-informed deep learning provides modest but measurable gains in brain metastasis segmentation, and these gains transfer across institutions: external validation on the Stanford BrainMetShare cohort (N = 105) showed that five of seven regularization weights significantly outperform the DynUNet baseline on tumor-core Dice (paired Wilcoxon p < 0.05), with the largest improvement of +10.1 % (p < 0.001) at λ = 0.001 and a ∼6× reduction in inter-fold variance at λ = 1.0. Optimal configuration varies by clinical application, informing context-specific deployment.

## 深度总结

**1. 研究背景与大问题**
神经肿瘤学中，检测、分割和勾画小体积脑转移灶仍是重大挑战。标准深度学习架构缺乏转移瘤生长和扩散的显式生物学信息，进一步限制了小体积转移灶的检测。物理信息（PI）方法能否克服这些尺寸依赖性限制值得探索。

**2. 具体研究问题**
（1）整合物理信息肿瘤生长模型能否改善脑转移瘤分割？（2）不同物理正则化权重如何影响不同组织类型和病灶尺寸的分割？（3）在不同临床场景（RANO评估、放疗计划、手术决策）中模型表现如何？

**3. 研究方法**
使用BraTS-METS 2023数据集，将基于物理的生长模型与DynUNet整合构建PI-DynUNet，在控制条件下与三种U-Net变体比较。所有模型使用相同数据训练，无数据增强，匹配参数量、相同超参数和确定性设置。比较7种物理正则化权重（λ），使用5折交叉验证，在6个病灶尺寸类别中评估Dice、IoU和HD95。计算RANO进展评估、放疗计划和手术决策的场景加权Dice系数。

**4. 主要发现**
PI-DynUNet在所有BraTS区域实现了有效分割。相比基线DynUNet，全肿瘤Dice提高1.8%，肿瘤核心Dice提高2.5%，增强肿瘤Dice提高2.6%。对于具有挑战性的非增强肿瘤核心（NETC），Dice提高5.3%。最优正则化权重取决于组织类型和病灶尺寸：λ=1.0有利于广泛水肿和全肿瘤区域，λ=0.01最适合大对比增强肿瘤和坏死核心。场景加权评估显示PI-DynUNet（λ=0.01）在增强加权场景中表现最佳（RANO: +2.6%; RT-GTV: +2.2%），λ=1.0在平衡准确率场景中表现更优（RT-CTV: +1.8%; 手术: +1.6%）。

**5. 结论与意义**
物理信息方法可有效改善脑转移瘤分割，特别是小体积病灶。不同临床场景需要不同的物理正则化策略，这为放疗计划中的GTV勾画提供了新思路。

**6. 局限性与展望**
仅在BraTS数据集上验证，需在临床数据上进一步测试。物理生长模型的简化假设可能限制泛化性。
